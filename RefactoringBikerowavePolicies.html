The goal of this rewrite is to give a better base on policies moving forward so that we’re all on the same page. This should also show some gaps in policy where we’re currently acting on something like the policy exists, when it’s actually just us being logical.

I made as minimal as possible changes to the actual writing of the policies. I removed policies that were found to have been proposed but not voted on or even failed/were tabled before voting. Several policies are noted as being severely obsolete, out of date or no longer followed. Certain policies were noted as needing a rewrite. I don’t want to have two people tell me different things about our policy, only to find it actually says a third.

Can we get this whole thing officially ratified at a member meeting? I’ll put up the draft at least 7 days before, tell people to read it over so they can bring up any issues with discussion on the forum, then vote to say that the current state of policy looks good. This also makes the transition to the new forum a lot easier since direct links to minutes may be lost and removes future issues of “well the old minutes didn’t say exactly blah blah blah.”

Proposal: starting with the May 2017 meeting, all policy must have a direct, full reference in the meeting minutes of the meeting where it occurred. For the future it is recommended that the person taking minutes writes down a proposal before it is voted and then reads it back before voting (remember to put vote totals next to proposals!). And it should go without saying but it’s only policy if a vote passes (that wasn’t always the case in the older policy notes). I (Lazar) will volunteer to take minutes at this next meeting and will write up a short list of the minimum things that we should expect a minute taker to handle.

Some of these things don’t need to be policy, because it’s moving things from one place to another. Just because it’s voted on doesn’t mean it has to be in here.

Policy: a course or principle of action to be taken in future recurring scenarios. “When ___ happens, then do ___”.

Action: an action to be completed, typically non-recurring. “We now have ___”.

The following is a collection of policies set forth at members’ meetings. All policies here were voted into practice by a quorum of the membership or board. Each policy must be followed by the date (MM.DD.YYYY) of the meeting(s) in which it was discussed, with a link to the meeting notes thread. Members’ meetings may be designated by MM preceding the date, board meetings are labeled with BM, combined board and members’ meetings are labeled with BMM.

Note that this is intended only as a record of policy passed at Bikerowave meetings and not as a volunteer handbook. All policies herein exist in conjunction with the bylaws. In case of conflict between this document and the meeting notes, the meeting notes take precedence.

Last updated for meeting in April 2017.

1. MEMBERSHIP STATUS AND BENEFITS

1.1 * (need to clarify) Volunteers under age 18 must have their parent come in and sign something or other (meeting notes don't say what), which must be witnessed and undersigned by the HM. MM.09.11.2011

1.2 * (is that membership at the turn of the month, getting 12 hours, or after being announced?) (the free stand time is in direct contradiction of the bylaws, which give it to provisional volunteers) Member Approval: Members must accumulate 12 hours of volunteering in one calendar month before becoming eligible for free stand time or any kind of discount. New members should be announced at members’ meetings. MM.06.05.2011.

1.3 Members are to pay 10% over wholesale for new parts from our suppliers. MM.11.07.2010.

1.4 OG status: Bikerowave OGs are not entitled to discounts on parts. They are entitled to free stand time. (if you have to ask how to get OG status you’re not eligible for it) MM.03.06.2011.

1.4b * (is this list still around?) A list of OG's will be posted behind the register, so that we know who they are. MM.08.12.2012.

1.5 * (do we do this anymore?) The list of current members entitled to discounts and free stand time should be made available near or via the POS. MM.09.05.2010.

1.6 * (this is mostly in the minutes but someone should rewrite this for clarity and to match what we really want) Members on sabbatical are counted as voting members for purposes of quorum only if they show up at meetings. Any volunteer with at least six consecutive months membership in good standing who drops below 12 hours retains their voting rights and membership benefits for six months. Any former volunteer who returns after their six-month sabbatical period is entitled to one month of membership (with associated voting rights and benefits) upon announcing their return. Thereafter said volunteer must sustain standard volunteer requirements. MM.11.13.2011 and MM.10.23.2011

1.7 Special Project status must be approved by the Board. MM.10.23.2011.

1.8 Wholesale purchasing partners: Full-time members of the CSUN Bike Collective may buy new items using our QBP account using the volunteer discount, provided that they establish one point person, and pay for the entire CSUNBC order promptly and together when they come in to pick them up, then divide the items amongst themselves. MM.05.20.2012. Bici Libre (MM.12.09.2012) and Optimist (MM.4.10.2016) may buy tools and shop supplies (not special orders for members) from us at wholesale prices.

1.9 Volunteers may earn volunteer hours by attending mechanical classes. This does not include non-mechanical classes. MM.06.10.2012.

1.10 Revocation of provisional volunteer status.

After an appropriate good faith effort to help the provisional volunteer fall in line has been made, any HM may initiate the revocation process by sending an email to the HM list stating their intent. If no HM objects within 48 hours, provisional volunteer status is revoked and the provisional volunteer may no longer volunteer for Bikerowave. The decision should be communicated to the former provisional volunteer as soon as possible.

Except in egregious cases, this process should only be used as a last resort after giving feedback and not seeing improvement. Ideally, an HM who objects should offer to allow the provisional volunteer to work on their shift.

Provisional membership may also be revoked by vote of the Board.

This process does not affect the ability of an HM to ban any volunteer from their shift. MM.06.10.2012.

1.11 Bikerowave anti-harassment policy.

Bikerowave expressly prohibits harassment by or of any volunteer or patron. Harassment includes, but is not limited to, physical violence, verbal abuse, insults, and sexual harassment. Harassment also includes behaviors which would cause a reasonable person to be uncomfortable being around the harasser or being part of Bikerowave.

Volunteers and members are expected to behave in accordance with the anti-harassment policy. Voted. MM.10.09.2016

1.12 * (should this be under the retail committee section?) Volunteers are expected to purchase special orders promptly. Volunteers may not place any further special orders until prior special orders have been returned or paid for; further special orders may be removed from the cart without notice by the retail committee. It is the ordering volunteer's responsibility to purchase (or return at their own expense) unwanted special orders (including items ordered by mistake or in the wrong quantity or due to website glitches). Exceptions may be made by the retail committee; this policy is not intended to make good-faith ordering difficult or to replace common sense. MM.12.11.2016

1.13 Items purchased with the discount are intended for personal use of the member only. Gifts are OK. Don’t resell items bought at discount. MM.08.12.2012.

1.14 New keyholders to the shop must be approved by the membership, unless time sensitive then the board can temporarily give a key. A temp keyholder must then be approved at the next member meeting to be a long term keyholder. MM.04.09.2017

2. STORAGE OF BIKES (DONATED AND CONSIGNMENT) AND PROPERTY

2.1 Volunteers may not store non-bike related belongings at Bikerowave. MM.06.05.2011.

2.2 Volunteers and customers may store one bike at Bikerowave for up to 30 days without being worked on. This includes bikes on consignment. Bikes should have name, phone number and date. MM.03.06.2011.

2.2b Members may temporarily store a second bike at Bikerowave for up to 14 days without being worked on. MM.04.03.2011

2.3 * (this needs to be re-written for clarity, and ideally we should just re-ratify this rather than having half a dozen different minutes linked) Consignment fee is 10% of bike’s selling price. MM.04.03.2011. Consignment fee applies when the sale takes place or is arranged at BRW, regardless of whether the item is "offered" for sale. Consignment fee does not apply if the sale is made off-site and BRW is merely the exchange point. Consignment fee does not apply for sales between volunteers. MM.08.12.2012. No bike may be sold through BRW without the HM initial and date on the checklist. Further, no HM may initial his or her own bike. MM.06.14.2015. Volunteers who engage in personal transactions (eg off-site Craigslist sales, spinlister) at Bikerowave must make it clear that they are engaging in a personal transaction and the shop is not involved; they must make sure the work does not spill over onto other volunteers; and if there is any question as to the propriety of what they are doing they should discuss it first with the Board or Membership.

2.4 Bikes on consignment have one month in which to be sold from Bikerowave and thus must be dated when first put up for sale in the shop. MM.04.03.2011.

2.6 * (this is a thing but not in those minutes) Volunteers should not label parts or frames as theirs unless they have paid for them. i.e. no holding parts. MM.09.05.2010.

2.6b Everything must be paid for in the same shift and there is to be no hoarding of unpaid-for parts in the back room. MM.04.04.2010.

2.7 * (we don’t have colored tags anymore, no mention of writing on tag in mm) Before a customer can store their bike at Bikerowave, a red tag must be placed on the bike with the customer’s contact information (name and phone number at minimum), and a corresponding card filled out and placed in the card box. MM.03.06.2011.

2.9 * (we tag things but in a different system than this) Tagging system for donated or for sale bikes: Red - bike is taken and has been paid for. MM.07.11.2010

2.10 * (no meeting notes indicated - should roll this in with tag/ceiling reform, it’s something we try to follow anyways) If a customer goes 30 days or more without working on their bike Bikerowave volunteers will make reasonable efforts to contact the owner before appropriating the bike.

2.11 * (our current action is to use the laminated sheet for sale bikes, donations just are tagged as such) Donated bikes should be tagged with a laminated white sheet that details the level of work done so far and that which remains to be done. MM.03.06.2011.

2.12 If junky shop bikes are to be stored outside, please lock them up in order to discourage a pattern of bike theft. MM.01.08.2012.

2.13 Food in the fridge should be labeled and dated. Food not labeled is up for grabs and may disappear on its own. A monthly fridge cleaning is desirable. MM.03.06.2011.

3. DURING SHIFTS

3.1 Shift reports should be posted on the forum before leaving the shop. MM.05.01.2011. Volunteers may use Google form to submit hours but HM’s should still post shift reports with a list of volunteers present and any notable issues. MM.3.13.2016.

3.2 Head mechanics and volunteers should arrive before doors open at 6:30pm on weekdays or 11am at the weekends. Head mechanics and volunteers must clean up after the shift is over. MM.03.06.2011.

3.2b * (do we do this at all?) HM's are not expected to open the shop if fewer than three volunteers are present at the shift. MM.09.09.2012.

3.3 * (this was proposed but unclear if even voted on, much less passed) One hour visits by volunteers shall not be counted towards volunteer hours for that month. MM.03.06.2011.

3.4 * (this was proposed but unclear if even voted on, much less passed) Volunteers should make an effort to notify Bikerowave when they are unable to attend their regular shift. Preferred method of contact is via the forum. MM.03.06.2011.

3.5 * (do we do this anymore?) Build a Bike Program. Participants will receive a frame or bike to work on and have one month to complete their project. Up to $70 will be awarded for the purchase of used parts to complete the bike. New parts may be allowed at the discretion of the head mechanic and the allocation of such parts must be noted on the shift report. MM.05.01.2011.

3.6 * (meeting notes indicate we’re not insured for e-bikes, someone may want to look into that to avoid any liability issues) E-bikes: E-bikes may be worked upon in the shop at the head mechanic’s discretion, and should be worked on if issue with bike is not related to the electric part eg the disk brakes. Insurance does not cover gas-powered or other fueled bikes and these must be turned away. MM.08.07.2011

3.7 Before a bike is taken down from the ceiling the area below should be cleared. BM.05.16.2010.

3.9 Customers are required to pay by the project, i.e. if two people are working on one bike, they are to be charged for one stand. BM.05.17.2009.

3.10 Customers that are unwilling to work on their own bike or are abusive towards volunteers qualify for automatic expulsion from Bikerowave. BMM.04.18.2010.

3.11 Volunteers may not ring themselves up. Volunteers may not price used parts for themselves. When ringing up used parts, you must always put a description in the comments. MM.02.09.2014.

3.12 All parts in display must be priced at fair retail value. MM.09.11.2011.

3.13 In order to keep an adequate stock of working used parts, patrons may not buy used parts unless they are to be installed on their own bikes within the shop. Exceptions to be made at HM's discretion; this policy is not intended to replace common sense. Policy does not apply to volunteers, but purchases must be within reason. MM.03.11.2012.

3.15 * (is this still a thing?) That Bikerowave continue the MVFM shift as before, with the understanding that Diana may pay a volunteer to work the shift. We agree that the equipment used and supplies expended are in support of BRW's outreach mission, and are not a personal benefit. Voted. MM.08.10.2013.

3.16 All dirty rags are to be stored in the red fire cans, not in open bins. Even if they’re only a little dirty. MM.04.10.2016.

3.17 Consumption of drugs and alcohol is banned inside the shop during open hours. MM.01.08.2012.

7. BOARD AND MEMBERS’ MEETINGS

7.1 In the event that a board member has to recuse themselves from a vote, the board has the option to appoint a voting member who may provide a 5th vote on motions. MM.12.06.2009.

7.2 In the event that more than one board member has to recuse themselves from a discussion then the membership shall take up the discussion and any subsequent votes. MM.12.06.2009.

7.3 Volunteers must be notified of the time and location of board or members’ meetings 14 days in advance, while the final agenda should be made public 7 days prior to the meeting. These stipulations do not supercede the bylaws. BM.05.17.2009.

7.4 In the event that an expenditure of greater than $250 is deemed necessary, a board member can make a motion to approve the expenditure, at which time the board will notify the volunteers. If the motion receives 4 out of 5 board members’ approval then the expenditure is approved and the volunteers will be notified of approval. BM.05.17.2009. Policy confirmed; requires board to seek consensus, just like every other decision we make. MM.10.23.2011.

7.5 Member meetings occur on the 2nd Sunday of each month, except for May when it is on the 3rd Sunday, at 6pm to avoid mother’s day conflict. MM.09.11.2011 and ammended MM.05.08.2016.

7.6 Bikerowave will subsidize food at member meetings, to a maximum of $150. MM.09.11.2011.

7.7 Members with pertinent, short factual information may move to the head of the stack by saying “point of fact.” MM.10.23.2011

7.8 The Chair chooses the order of items for each meeting. Members posting multiple items should indicate which they consider most important. MM.10.23.2011.

7.8b The Chair is encouraged to track how much time has been spent on each agenda item and to announce the time spent at 15-minute intervals. MM.08.12.2012.

7.9 * (we do this but no mm indicated) Items may be added to a meeting agenda for discussion only (no vote) after the close of the agenda. These items will be heard after all timely items.

7.10 * (we do this but no mm indicated) The first item on each meeting agenda will be a motion to approve the minutes from the previous meeting. A "for" vote means only that you agree that the minutes accurately reflect whan took place at the meeting, not that you necessarily agree with the decisions made.

7.11 Meetings shall be limited to 2 hours excluding eating time. Any items not gotten to must be re-proposed for future meetings. MM.12.11.2016

7.12 * (unclear if this actually passed or was implemented) At the start of each member meeting, after the initial go round we instate a meeting facilitator through a vote. Anyone can propose themselves to be the facilitator or decline to play the role if they are voted into the position. The facilitator is in charge of leading discussions, making sure all sides are heard and ensuring more verbose members are not dominating the conversation and quieter members have a chance and are encouraged to share their thoughts. this does not mean necessarily keeping a strict stack. The facilitator does not keep stack, someone else does. The facilitator orders the agenda items in order using what they think is in the best interest of the co-op as their guiding principle. Members do have the right to speak up if they feel an item is too far down on the list and we can vote to move it higher. The membership is encouraged to keep objections to a minimum and only make them if they feel there is a very pressing need to have the item discussed sooner. MM.12.11.2016

Addendum: What Does Clean Mean at the BRW?

Daily:

  1. All tools are clean and in their respective spots
  2. Counter tops are clean and clear
  3. Floor is swept behind the counter, in the patron area and in the office
  4. No food for humans or pets is left out
  5. Trash from all bins, including bathroom and behind the counter is taken to the dumpster
  6. The parts washer has no parts or tools left in it.
  7. No items (parts, hardware, bikes, etc.) are left on the bike stands.
  8. Personal property is taken home.
  9. Bikes are tagged and either properly stored or on the window sill for sale
  10. Donated bikes are clearly labeled as donations
  11. All dishes are cleaned and put away in the kitchen area

Once every month:

  1. Clean the bathroom thoroughly
  2. Clean the kitchen appliances (fridge, microwave, etc.)
  3. Vacuum the lounge
  4. Sweep under the work benches
  5. Clean the shop windows
